Soil Distribution in Punjab

Punjab Soil and Crops

Soil in Punjab

Punjab primarily has Alluvial Soil, highly fertile due to the rivers Sutlej, Beas, and Ravi.

Types of Soil in Punjab

Alluvial Soil

  • Location: Ludhiana, Jalandhar, Amritsar, Patiala
  • Characteristics: Rich in potash, phosphoric acid, lime; deficient in nitrogen, organic matter
  • Suitable Crops: Wheat, Rice, Sugarcane, Pulses, Oilseeds, Fruits, Vegetables

Loamy and Clayey Soil

  • Location: Regions with irrigation facilities
  • Suitable Crops: Cotton, Barley, Maize

Irrigation in Punjab

Punjab benefits from an extensive canal system and tube wells, supported by the Bhakra-Nangal Dam.

Crop Rotation in Punjab

  • Wheat-Rice Rotation: Wheat (Rabi) and Rice (Kharif)
  • Maize-Potato Rotation: Maize (Summer) and Potato (Winter)

Challenges

  • Decreasing soil fertility due to overuse of fertilizers
  • Salinity and waterlogging
  • Soil erosion from over-irrigation
